Entertainment Around New Bedford

  • Home

New Bedford has several small museums, an burgeoning art scene, a tall ship, and plenty of beaches to explore.

Arthur Moniz Art Gallery

Art gallery located across the street from the Whaling Museum contains paintings, line art, and other work by a local New Bedford artist. Mr. Moniz’s art focuses on the beauty of the historic maritime coastal communities of the region.

Harborwalk

The newly installed Harbor Walk gives visitors a chance to walk across the city’s hurricane barrier, and look out upon where Buzzards Bay meets historic Fort Taber, beyond to Fairhaven’s Fort Phoenix, West Island, and noted points of interest like Butler Flats Lighthouse. The elevated walking trail stretches 3880 ft. (.735 mi) and is lit at night, making any time a good time to take in the natural wonder of South Coast, MA.

Seamen’s Bethel

Built in 1832, the Seamen's Bethel was specifically constructed for the many sailors who called New Bedford their home port, who considered it a matter of tradition that one visited the chapel before setting sail.

New Bedford Whaling National Historical Park

New Bedford Whaling National Historical Park is located in downtown New Bedford. The park is located within a 13-block historic district of cobblestone streets. Start your visit at the visitors’ center to pick up a map, watch the 20-minute orientation movie, and take in the exhibits about whaling and New Bedford, “The City That Lit the World.”
